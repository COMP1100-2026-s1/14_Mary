# MVP Test and Learning Cards

## MVP Hypothesis

**HYPOTHESIS**: We believe that Engineering, Architecture and Information Technology (EAIT) students would benefit from a streamlined, elegantly designed course-selection webapp that simplifies and collates all relevant course details (especially pertaining to course pre-requisites and future courses possiblities), provides yearly suggested course structures for each degree, allows for users to search courses by keywords in the course details, and provides ways for users to publicly rate and review courses they have undertaken.

**TESTS**: While we have generally gotten the information required from previous tests, the following questions pertain more specifically to gauging user interest in our prospective features.

1. How would you rate the general difficulty and intuitiveness in selecting your courses at UQ?
2. Did you find it particularly unintuitive to navigate course pre-requisites?
3. How would it have benefited you to have the course pre-requisites, as well as the future courses it was a pre-requisite for listed cleanly?
4. How would it have benefitted you to have viewable pre-set course structures for your degree?
5. What issues did you have with the current UQ course search system?
6. Did you try to find ratings and reviews for the courses you were undertaking online?

**METRIC**: We will pull from both previous and new data to analyse the most frequently discussed pain points, along with the most frequently requested features that interviewees mentioned. Questions such as the ones listed above, as well as question 1 and 4 from the Hypothesis 4 test cards and question 6 from the Hypothesis 3 test card are examples of questions with data that would be the most assistive in measurably assessing the relevance of our proposed features to student grievances and desires.

**CRITERIA**: The hypothesis holds true if both the most commonly reported feature requests and pain points for students aligns with the features proposed in our hypothesis, namely:

- A simplified course details layout
- Pre-made course templates
- Keyword-based course searching system
- User Ratings for Courses

## MVP Learning Card

- We ultimately believe that not only do the majority of UQ students have issues with the course selection process, as proven in our previous testing, but the most commonly reported issues with the course selection process could be resolved with a webapp that simplifies the layout of individual course details, provides pre-made course templates for students according to their degree structure, provides a keyword-based searching system (especially relevant for electives), and a way for students to rate and review courses, as well as view other student's ratings.

- We observed numerous reports of the exact same fundamental issues, with student's responses hardly deviating from the 4 most commonly reiterated pain points (and respective feature requests).
