# Jack Cole Interveiw 1
[Link to recording on MS teams](https://uq.sharepoint.com/:u:/r/teams/Section_7620_22283/Shared%20Documents/14_Mary/jack%20interview%201%20iteration2.m4a?csf=1&web=1&e=zrSPVl)

_What did the main purpose of this program appear to be to you, as you were using it?_\
When I looked through the app it appeared to be a degree planning tool that offers, um, extended info on courses. Like reviews, things like that, like a modern version of our course system that also has like, degree plans.\
_Did you find it easy to navigate?_\
Yes, very easy and the details of each page were well mapped out and kind of, clear I guess. Yeah just very easy.\
_How much more useful would you say it was, to have the full course plans placed on an app with the individual course details, rather than having the information scattered as it is currently?_\
I would say it was a lot more useful, and I really liked how the course plan screen like, linked to the details screen, so if I clicked say the first course on the plan, it would instantly link me to the details of the course, so it’s all in one kind of space. I found it a lot easier having it all in one space.\
_If this app were to be released publicly, what do you think it’s main advantage compared to the current system would be?_\
It would just make the system, like the course structure a lot clearer I guess, which would help a lot of students, including myself to be able to like, have a more obvious grasp of not only the paths we are intended to follow for our degrees, but also the deeper details on the courses and what life as a student in doing that courses is like. It also kind of brings it all together, having a plan given and then being able to see like, why I’m doing each course.\
_Do you mean with the future courses section?_\
Yes, with the future courses it kind of helps to bring the degree together. I guess that’s what I was trying to say um, with earlier, with having it all in one space. Having all those different screens together makes a lot more sense and would benefit a lot of students, especially those who are bit more unsure regarding like why they’re doing this, like this course, or what it will be like.\
_Could you imagine this tool saving students a lot of time?_\
Yes, especially the earlier students who are in say, their first year or second year, or whatever.\
_Earlier you mentioned our implementation would provide a better look into the student’s life in this course, were you referring to the reviews section?_\
Yeah, the reviews are something I always thought was really missing, but maybe it should have like, a moderation system or clearing system or something, so only the high reviews make it. But yeah, it’s always good to hear from fellow students to know like, what a course is like, the course details can only kind of tell you so much, that’s why I always have to ask like older students and I would much rather just have it like easily accessible to look when I’m looking at everything else and trying to plan, or navigate everything else that’s um, around.\
_Was that the only way you had to find out information prior?_\
Well, I would look it up online but again, that’s not the same as like, you know..\
_Like having it all collated in one like we are_\
Yeah like collated, and like I just instantly know. I don’t have to like put in 10 screens, or tabs in Google and like, be searching all this stuff when it’s all in one main space.\
_Is there any information you wish you could see in the system that wasn’t in the MVP?_\
Maybe like, exam stuff if you can get a hold of that.\
_Such as what in particular would you like?_\
Just like, the percentage, like the assessments page on our course profile, to get all that information because a lot of students wonder whether like, this is an assignment heavy course or an exam heavy- but I guess you have the ratings, but yeah, like some official data would be good to see.\
_Did you like the design of the website, in terms of how it looks and how it feels to navigate it?_\
Yes, I liked the green, but maybe if it’s for UQ um, I don’t know it was fine to use I thought it was good. Nothing was confusing and the links were like, good like I was saying yeah.\
_So right now you seem pretty optimistic on your potential to use this app?_\
Yeah definitely, or especially like, if I could go back in time to my first semester first year or something.\
_How would you feel if we monetised it with ads? Like say, a banner on the bottom of the course details pages or something._\
Um, I guess I wouldn’t really care because I have an adblocker. So um, I wouldn’t really care.\
_What if you hadn’t had an adblocker?_\
Um, I don’t know, I can’t really imagine it. I’ve always had it so I never really care if a website or something has like, ads because um, it never really affects me.\
_Thank you very much for your time, I appreciate it a lot, have a good day_\
Thanks, you too.\
