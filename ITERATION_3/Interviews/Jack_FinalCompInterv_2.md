_After going through and briefly using our product, what do you think it is trying to help you as a UQ student with?_
I think it’s mainly trying to, um, simplify course selection and planning for your degrees, it kind of pulls it all together into one place.
_So like a planning tool, that’s all in one?_
Yes, especially for mapping out your degree across different semesters, and seeing course details and that.
_Do you find it more useful seeing a full course plan based on your degree structure alongside course details and everything else, rather than having to you know hunt it down yourself?_
Yeah, that’s actually really useful. At the moment I usually just try to manually map it by looking at the UQ course prerequisites, having it visually laid out means I wouldn’t miss my pre-reqs or, like, accidentally plan something in the wrong semester. It’s just more structure, structured.
_Do you think that would primarily benefit you by saving time, or just providing more clarity?_
I guess both but probably on the clarity side would be more, um, relevant, I guess to reduce mistakes while planning.
_Do you find it valuable being able to see not just the prereqs for a course but what is is a pre-req for, like what it leads to?_
Yes, normally I only see them, like going backwards, like the prerequs needed, so seeing future, um, dependencies helps you under stand the consequences of not taking a course or taking it or whatever. Like if I want to skip something, I can kind of the see idea, of what it’s for and decide you know, whether or not I should skip it, or if I will need it in a few semesters or years time.
_So overall, this tool could assist you with decision making?_
Yeah absolutely.
_So, you saw the reviews and ratings section?_
Yeah I had a look, on the um, course details.
_Would you find that helpful for making the course selection process a bit clearer?_
Yeah absolutely, it would be nice to see real experiences like in the same place as the general details, because otherwise I had to put it like on Google and go to, um, like random stuff, and to have it on the same place where I like look at the course and see what it entails both now and in the future is highly beneficial.
_Is there any information you wish you could see in the system that wasn’t in the MVP?_
Well I wanted to take MATH1071 but couldn’t because it was um, in this semester not the next one, so I was thinking maybe if you had the semester’s the courses run because I hadn’t seen that, or if I did I just missed it I don’t know.
_No no, that’s a good point. Not something I’ve thought of. Do you find the design intuitive, and the experience of like, clicking the buttons and navigating the different pages to be quite natural?_
Yeah, just like any other website really, not like cluttered or anything, which I like. Like, the current one from um, UQ is like a mess really and really cluttered. It was nice that it wasn’t like cluttered or, messy or too much or anything. Just plain, but still nice to look at.
_Like minimalist?_
Yeah, like tasteful, haha, I don’t know.
_If we were to monetise the product with ads in the future, how would you, as a user feel about that? How much would it detract from the experience?_
I guess a fair bit, not really in the, um, functionality but just in the, how annoying it is like to be using it and then ads come up. Would it be everywhere?
_No, it wouldn’t like block anything._
I guess it wouldn’t be too bad but just a bit annoying. I mean, I would still use it.
_Yeah._
But it would lessen the experience still, yeah. But not like, massively.
_Well thank you for the time, that wraps up the interview, and yeah, have a good one, thanks again._
Thank you, cya.
