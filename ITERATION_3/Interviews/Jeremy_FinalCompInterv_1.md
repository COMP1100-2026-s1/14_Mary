**Interviewer:** Okay, yeah. I'm happy you got that from the demo. How useful does the idea of seeing a full course plan based on your degree feel to you? \

**Participant:** Very, very valuable. \
**Interviewer:** Can you touch on whether that would have been useful from personal experience back when you were first starting uni? \
**Participant:** Yes, 100%. When I was trying to transfer through multiple degrees, there were always times where I wondered how much credit I could get for one specific course. Do I still have to do this course? Is this course a prerequisite to that course? By having it all at a glance, it makes everything so much easier. I don’t have to go back and forth with the university or email different faculties asking whether something is a prerequisite or whether credit transfers across. It’s very valuable. \
**Interviewer:** Okay. How helpful do the course reviews and recommendations seem in helping you choose between different courses? \
**Participant:** Hmm. I think it’s alright, actually. Obviously it’s helpful, but I’m a person who puts everything into AI anyway. I ask a lot of questions because I like making well-informed and educated decisions. Even though seeing course reviews and recommendations would help me make a decision, most of the time when I find a course I’m already aware of what I’m getting into. So for me it’s alright, but I’m glad it’s a feature for people who need it. \

**Participant:** Off the top of my head, not really. Maybe there could be some UI improvements, but those are more late-stage things. For an early demo, I’m pretty happy with what I see. \
