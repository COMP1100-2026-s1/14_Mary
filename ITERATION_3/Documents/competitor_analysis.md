| Competitor | Target Market | Key Value Propositions | Weaknesses | Notes
| ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- |

| Official UQ System | UQ students with institutional login credentials, requiring access to formal and informal UQ information sources | Institutional authority and data integrity | a. Course information is dispersed across multiple platforms, often presented inconsistently or incompletely. 
b. No centralised repository for course requirements and details; students must navigate multiple pages to review individual courses. 
c. No standardised format for course detail presentation; writing style and information structure varies significantly across courses. 
d. Minimal immediate feedback when incorrect courses are selected, with limited corrective guidance available outside of direct consultation with an academic advisor. |
| AI Language Models | Students with limited time or familiarity with official UQ resources and their information architecture | Broad, accessible knowledge synthesised and delivered in a tailored format | a. Model outputs are constrained by the scope and recency of training data, which is fixed as of the model's last update. 
b. AI-generated outputs carry an inherent risk of hallucination, potentially producing inaccurate or fabricated course information that could result in non-viable academic plans. 
c. Recommendations may be based on surface-level information, failing to account for nuances and institutional complexities that official sources and course coordinators are better positioned to address. 
d. Response or context limitations may result in incomplete consideration of all enrolment requirements, producing conflicting or non-compliant course plans. | 
| Personal Notes & Scheduling Tools | Students with strong information-gathering skills who prefer manual control over their academic planning | Unconstrained, fully customisable planning environment | a. Requires manual entry and organisation of all relevant data. 
b. Necessitates sourcing information manually from the same fragmented platforms as other alternatives. 
c. Data accuracy is entirely dependent on the individual's own verification process, introducing risk if inaccurate information is incorporated into the plan. 
d. The quality and usability of the resulting plan is contingent on the individual's document formatting and organisational capabilities. | 
| Peers & Senior Students | Students who have access to contacts with direct experience in the same or similar degree programs, whose firsthand knowledge can inform course planning | Practical guidance grounded in lived academic experience | a. Entirely contingent on the availability of suitably experienced contacts within the student's personal network. 
b. Advice quality is dependent on the accuracy of the contact's recollection and interpretation of their own experience, which may not reliably reflect current program requirements. 
c. Contacts may lack the ability to communicate course information clearly or precisely, introducing risks around presentation and data integrity independent of factual accuracy. |
