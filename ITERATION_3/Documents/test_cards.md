# Test Cards

## Hypothesis 1

**HYPOTHESIS**: We believe that UQ students have issues during the course selection process which could be amended by us using technology. 

**TEST**: To verify that, we will ask existing students about their experiences with course selection at UQ with an emphasis on any issues that they have encountered during the process and any features they wish had existed while completing it. 

**METRIC**: And measure the amount of UQ students who agree that they had significant issues during the course selection process. 

**CRITERIA**: We are right if the amount of students who admit to having issues of any severity is greater than the amount of students who claim to have no issue. 

**INTERVIEW QUESTIONS**
1. What are some of the issues you have encountered with the current UQ enrolment system? 

2. What do you find confusing about picking your courses at UQ? 

3. What information do you wish you had when you first began picking your courses? 

4. What information sources have you sought when selecting courses? 

5. What do you consider when deciding your electives? 

6.  Based on your past experiences with enrolling, what are some features that weren’t present or well implemented, that you desired or felt that an enrolment system should have had? 

7. Did you feel that the enrolment system is responsive enough in sending feedback, confirmation and/or warnings when selecting courses, and if not, what kinds of feedback or responses would you feel would provide sufficient confirmation or prevent uncertainty after clicking a button?

## Hypothesis 3

**HYPOTHESIS**: We believe that a major and surprisingly common pain point UQ students regarding the UQ enrolment system is the lack of context and information when selecting courses, or at least a lack of convenience and clarity when looking up the appropriate sources. 

**TEST**: To validate our hypothesis, we will ask UQ students specifically and more in-depth questions about
* What their current information sources for selecting courses are, and if they are provided, endorsed or recommended by UQ, or third-party external resources
* If their current UQ-affiliated sources are satisfactory or sufficient in the information/context/instruction they provide
* If their current non-UQ-affiliated sources (if any) are satisfactory or sufficient in the information/context/instruction they provide
* How their current personal enrollment process- or mind- flow goes, and what sources/sites they reference for information
* What their first enrollment process was like, and how they found or experimented with the proper steps & sources to complete the enrollment process and select their courses correctly
* If they have any particular bad experiences, mistakes or better information sources, that they would like to see changes to UQ's current system to be based on

**METRIC**: We will assess the stated quality of both UQ and non-UQ sources of information students use, and see what exactly it is about UQ's system or the external sources that make them dissatisfied with the former or more inclined towards the latter

**CRITERIA**: Our hypothesis is valid if three out of the five students we inteview affirm that there are indeed weaknesses about the current UQ enrollment or information system, and/or if there are external sources that address common issues students want to avoid with the current official system.

**INTERVIEW QUESTIONS**
1.Could you briefly summarise your experience in choosing your courses at UQ for the very first time?

2.Which parts of the course choosing process did you find challenging?

3.In what ways did the organization of information on various web pages impact your experience?

4.Which information regarding courses was hard to locate or understand, for instance, workload or course difficulty level?
